How the 0–100 score works

The score measures how much chemistry data is present and how rich it is. A high score means the COA is comprehensive. A low score means data is missing or thin. It is not a judgment of the product — it is a measure of the document.

25 pts Potency — THC/CBD levels relative to product type (flower, concentrate, CBD)
25 pts Terpenes — total terpene content and number of compounds identified
20 pts Minor cannabinoids — breadth of the full cannabinoid panel beyond THC/CBD
20 pts Safety — which contaminant panels are tested and whether they passed
10 pts Data completeness — key fields present: batch, lab, date, accreditation

Important Disclaimers & Limitations

Not medical advice. Alem Chemical Intelligence reports are generated exclusively from the chemical data contained within the submitted Certificate of Analysis. All analysis, scoring, terpene characterisations, cannabinoid descriptions, and effect direction inferences are informational in nature and do not constitute medical advice, clinical recommendations, or therapeutic guidance. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before making any decisions about medicinal cannabis use, prescription, or dosage adjustment.

Chemistry only. This report reflects the chemistry documented in the submitted COA at the time of laboratory testing. It does not account for individual patient physiology, tolerance, consumption method, product batch variation, storage conditions post-testing, or any other factor that may influence therapeutic outcomes. The same chemical profile may produce very different effects in different individuals.

Effect direction is observational, not clinical. Terpene-based effect direction inferences (e.g. "Uplifting / mentally active") are derived from observational correlations between terpene chemotypes and self-reported experiences. They are not based on randomised controlled trials conducted on this specific product. They represent a directional, population-level signal — not a personal clinical prediction.

Safety data limitation. If a safety panel is listed as "Not Tested" in this report, it means that data was not present in the submitted COA. This is not a confirmation of product safety. Products without pesticide, heavy metal, or microbial testing should not be assumed compliant. Always request complete safety documentation from your producer or pharmacist before use.
